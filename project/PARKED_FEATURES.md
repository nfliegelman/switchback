# PARKED_FEATURES.md

Updated 2026-07-20 per project/MASTER_COURSE_CORRECTION.md section on
work to stop or park. Nothing here is deleted; classification decides
what sessions may spend time on. Four classes:

- PRESERVED FOUNDATION: keep working, keep tested, build on it.
- EXPERIMENTAL: exists, honestly labeled, not in the primary flow.
- PARKED: do not build without new evidence or an owner decision.
- LATER-PHASE: real opportunity, sequenced behind the core journey.

## Preserved foundation

The solver, graph, and availability layers (including dual-inventory
MAX-merge and mixed permit policies). Trail geometry, DEM grading,
toughest-stretch profiles, GPX and CalTopo export. The watch machine,
Telegram path, board cron, and scan history (substrate for future
complete-trip alerts). The calibration scaffold and ballots (owner
reactions still wanted). The 72-area atlas, boundary registry, and
three long-trail corridors (frozen assets; they power editor legality
coloring and permit-free fallbacks later). The tkinter GUI as an
admin and spreadsheet utility. The .bat launchers and OWNER.md as the
owner's only executable test harness.

## Experimental

Dispersed-camp candidate generation (oracle-gated; failed pilots get
deleted whole). Cross-boundary region overlays beyond Lena.
Point-to-point vocabulary in the engine (the solver still closes
every trip at its starting entrance; no UI may imply otherwise).

## Parked

Additional park, region, or long-trail expansion, including the
Archetype B Sierra trailhead-quota architecture. Generic campground
cancellation alerts and raw inventory scanning as a headline feature.
Competing with AllTrails, CalTopo, Gaia, Garmin, onX, or FarOut on
navigation. Generic road-trip planning and POI tours. New map layers
without a demonstrated trip-planning use. Solitude modeling (data
source exists; recommendation quality is not calibrated yet).
Recommendation personalization, social feeds, gamification,
monetization infrastructure (gates archived in
project/archive/FUTURE.md). Cosmetic version-number releases.
Documentation reorganizations that do not enable implementation.

## Later-phase

Three to five trip-ready regions, cross-destination and flexible-date
search, drive-radius search, saved preference profiles, trip-level
availability monitoring, route repair, camp substitution, direction
reversal, basecamp conversion, better frontcountry options with live
inventory, Campflare handoff links, formal partner integrations,
road-corridor backpacking discovery ("what viable trip lies along
this driving route", never a generic Roadtrippers competitor).
