## What this project is

Switchback: a permit-aware backpacking trip finder built on recreation.gov data. The name is the trail's answer to terrain that is too steep for one day, which is what the solver does to itineraries. It started as a Tkinter GUI that dumps wilderness permit availability to Excel and is growing into: a constraint-based itinerary recommender (daily miles, gain, grade bounds), an interactive "adventure mode" frontier explorer, camp ratings, and GPX or CalTopo handoff. Owner: Noah, construction estimator who has shipped his own quant models with GitHub Actions and Pages dashboards, but who for Switchback is treated as a non-coder end user, the delivery premise stated in CLAUDE.md and PRODUCT.md that governs every interface decision: he drives the app from the phone board and the double-click .bat launchers. Plans PNW and Rockies trips.

- 2026-07-20 (grade-aware pace, owner-requested same day): the owner hiked 45 percent grade sections and correctly observed one such mile dominates a flat day; switchback/pace.py now models it.
